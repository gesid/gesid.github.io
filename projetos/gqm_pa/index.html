GQM+PA: A Framework based on Goal-Question-Metric and Action Research for Teaching Software Measurement

Saori Costa, Federal University of Ceará Crateús, Ceará, Brazil, saoricosta@gmail.com.br

1 - Abstract

Software measurement is a crucial practice in software quality assurance, being intrinsically related to the evaluation of software metrics. However, the definition of which metrics should be evaluated in a software project is a complex task, thus requiring decision support methods. Among the usually addressed methods in software projects, one can highlight the Goal-Question-Metric (GQM) that aims to align the definition of metrics to the objectives of the organization. Furthermore, the understanding and execution of the processes that involve software measurement are closely linked to the level of qualification of the professional that performs it. Hence, there is an on-going challenge in teaching software measurement by addressing theory and practice to aware the software engineering students for a well-prepared professional career. Therefore, we propose a framework named as GQM+PA for teaching software measurement based upon the integration of the Action Research methodology to the GQM process. Regarding the empirical evaluation, we investigated through a quali-quantitative perspective multiple case studies in the Federal University of Ceará (\textit{Campus} Crateús). Our major contributions are summarized as follows: i) the proposal of an integrated framework for theoretical and practical learning of software measurement and ii) all routines, dashboards, and spreadsheets may be adapted and evolved by other projects as organizational assets.

Keywords: Software Quality, Software Measurement, GQM, Action Research, Software Teaching.
