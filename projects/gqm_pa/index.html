GQM+PA: A Framework based on Goal-Question-Metric and Action Research for Teaching Software Measurement

Saori Costa, Federal University of Ceará, Crateús, Ceará, Brazil, saoricosta@gmail.com.br

Allysson Allex Araújo, Federal University of Ceará Crateús, Ceará, Brazil, allysson.araujo@crateus.ufc.br

Abstract Questionários Entrevista Planilhas Projetos Monografia

1 - Abstract

Software measurement is a crucial practice in software quality assurance and is intrinsically associated with software metric assessment. However, the software metrics definition is a complex task, thus requiring decision support methods. Among the methods usually approached, one can highlight the Goal-Question-Metric (GQM) that aligns the definition of metrics to the objectives of the organization. In addition, the understanding of software processes is closely linked to different situational characteristics, including previous experience. Hence, there is an on-going challenge in teaching software measurement, addressing theory and practical experience to make software engineering students aware of a well-prepared career. Therefore, we propose a framework called GQM+PA for teaching software measurement based on the integration of the Action Research method with the GQM process. Regarding the empirical evaluation, we investigated multiple case studies at the Federal University of Ceará (Campus Crateús) through a quali-quantitative perspective. Results reveal that the students were able to improve their knowledge and empirically perceive the value of software measurement, including basic concepts, metrics and GQM.

Keywords: Software Quality, Software Measurement, GQM, Action Research, Software Teaching.
